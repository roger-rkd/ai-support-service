NHS SERVICES AND APPOINTMENTS GUIDE

## HOW TO BOOK A GP APPOINTMENT

Methods to book:
1. **Phone your GP surgery** - quickest method
   - Call during surgery hours (usually 8am-6:30pm Mon-Fri)
   - Have your NHS number ready if possible
   - Explain your symptoms to receptionist

2. **Online booking** - if your surgery offers it
   - Register for online services through your GP
   - Book appointments 24/7
   - Check test results and order prescriptions

3. **In person** - visit reception
   - Go to your GP surgery during opening hours
   - Speak to reception staff

## TYPES OF GP APPOINTMENTS

**Same-day/urgent appointments:**
- For urgent medical problems that can't wait
- Call your GP surgery first thing in the morning
- Explain it's urgent to the receptionist
- You may be offered a phone consultation first

**Routine appointments:**
- For non-urgent health concerns
- May be booked days or weeks in advance
- Face-to-face or telephone consultation

**Phone consultations:**
- GP calls you at appointed time
- Good for minor issues, follow-ups, prescription queries
- May lead to face-to-face appointment if needed

**Video consultations:**
- Some surgeries offer video calls
- Useful if you can't leave home
- You can show visible symptoms

## FINDING YOUR NEAREST GP

How to find a GP:
1. Visit NHS website: www.nhs.uk/service-search/find-a-gp
2. Enter your postcode
3. View list of nearby practices with:
   - Distance from you
   - Contact details
   - Opening hours
   - Services offered

Registering with a GP:
- You can register with any GP surgery
- Don't need proof of address, immigration status, or ID
- Fill in registration form (GMS1)
- May need to have a health check with practice nurse

## NHS 24 (CALL 111)

When to use NHS 24:
- Urgent medical help needed but not emergency
- Your GP surgery is closed
- You're not sure which NHS service you need
- Need advice about what to do next

What NHS 24 can help with:
- Assess your symptoms
- Give self-care advice
- Direct you to appropriate service
- Arrange appointment with GP or nurse
- Prescribe medication if needed

Available:
- 24 hours a day, 365 days a year
- Call 111 (free from landline and mobile)

## A&E (ACCIDENT & EMERGENCY)

When to go to A&E (or call 999):
- Severe chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Heavy bleeding that won't stop
- Severe allergic reaction
- Severe burns or scalds
- Stroke symptoms (FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech problems, Time to call 999)
- Suspected broken bones
- Head injuries with loss of consciousness
- Seizures/fits

What to expect:
- Triage nurse will assess urgency
- You may wait (unless life-threatening)
- Treatment based on clinical priority, not arrival time

## WALK-IN CENTRES AND MINOR INJURIES UNITS

For minor illnesses and injuries:
- Cuts requiring stitches
- Sprains and strains
- Minor infections
- Rashes
- Coughs and colds

Benefits:
- No appointment needed
- Usually open longer hours than GP
- Experienced healthcare professionals

Find your nearest:
- Visit NHS website
- Call NHS 24 on 111
- Google "walk-in centre near me"

## PHARMACY (CHEMIST)

Pharmacists can help with:
- Minor ailments (cough, cold, sore throat)
- Advice on medicines
- Emergency contraception
- Vaccinations (flu jab, etc.)
- Blood pressure checks
- Minor wound care

Services:
- Many offer private consultation rooms
- No appointment needed
- Can recommend over-the-counter medicines
- Can refer to GP if needed

## REPEAT PRESCRIPTIONS

How to order:
1. **Online** - if registered for online services
2. **Phone** - call your GP surgery
3. **In person** - drop slip at surgery
4. **Pharmacy** - some collect repeat prescriptions

Allow 48 hours for processing.

## DENTAL EMERGENCIES

For dental problems:
- Contact your regular dentist first
- If no dentist or surgery closed, call NHS 24 on 111
- They can find emergency dentist
- A&E only for facial trauma/swelling affecting breathing

## HOME VISITS

Your GP may visit if:
- You're too ill to travel
- You have serious disability
- You're elderly and frail

To request:
- Call your GP surgery before 10:30am if possible
- Explain why you can't come to surgery
- Reception will assess urgency

Note: Home visits are for those genuinely unable to attend surgery.

## GETTING MEDICAL HELP WHEN AWAY FROM HOME

In Scotland:
- You can see any GP if visiting another area
- Tell reception you're a temporary resident
- May be charged for prescriptions in England/Wales

Abroad:
- Get European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) for EU travel
- Consider travel insurance
- Call your travel insurance emergency line

REMEMBER: For life-threatening emergencies, always call 999. For urgent medical advice, call NHS 24 on 111. For routine care, contact your GP surgery.
