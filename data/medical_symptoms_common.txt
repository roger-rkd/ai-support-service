COMMON SYMPTOMS AND HOME REMEDIES

## FEVER

What is a fever?
A fever is when your body temperature is 38°C (100.4°F) or higher. It's usually a sign your body is fighting an infection.

When to worry:
- Call 999 if temperature is above 40°C (104°F) and not coming down
- See a GP if fever lasts more than 3 days
- Seek immediate help for babies under 3 months with fever above 38°C

Home treatment:
1. Rest and drink plenty of fluids
2. Take paracetamol or ibuprofen to reduce temperature
3. Keep room at comfortable temperature (not too hot or cold)
4. Remove excess clothing
5. Use a cool (not cold) flannel on forehead

## HEADACHE

Common causes:
- Dehydration
- Stress and tension
- Eye strain
- Lack of sleep
- Sinus problems

Home treatment:
1. Drink plenty of water
2. Rest in a quiet, dark room
3. Take paracetamol or ibuprofen
4. Apply cool compress to forehead
5. Gentle neck and shoulder massage
6. Avoid screens for a while

When to seek help:
- Call 999: sudden severe headache (worst ever), headache after head injury, headache with vision problems or confusion
- See GP: frequent headaches, headaches lasting more than a few days

## COUGH

When it's usually harmless:
- Coughs often accompany colds and flu
- Usually clears up within 2-3 weeks

Home treatment:
1. Stay hydrated - warm drinks help soothe
2. Honey and lemon in hot water (not for babies under 1)
3. Rest and keep head slightly elevated when sleeping
4. Breathe in steam (not for young children)
5. Use over-the-counter cough medicine (check with pharmacist)

See a doctor if:
- Coughing up blood
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Cough lasts more than 3 weeks
- You have a weakened immune system

## COLD AND FLU

Symptoms:
Cold: runny nose, sore throat, cough, sneezing
Flu: sudden fever, aches, tiredness, dry cough

Home treatment:
1. Rest and sleep
2. Keep warm
3. Drink plenty of water, juice, or soup
4. Gargle salt water for sore throat (1 tsp salt in warm water)
5. Take paracetamol or ibuprofen for aches and fever

When to see a doctor:
- Symptoms worsen or don't improve after a week
- High fever that doesn't respond to treatment
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe chest pain
- You're pregnant, elderly, or have underlying health conditions

## SORE THROAT

Home remedies:
1. Gargle warm salt water (not for children)
2. Drink plenty of cool or warm fluids
3. Suck on ice cubes, ice lollies, or hard sweets
4. Avoid smoking or smoky places
5. Use paracetamol or ibuprofen

See a GP if:
- Sore throat doesn't improve after a week
- You often get sore throats
- You have a high fever
- You have white patches on tonsils
- You're worried about your symptoms

## STOMACH ACHE

Common causes:
- Indigestion
- Constipation
- Period pains
- Food poisoning
- Trapped wind

Home treatment:
1. Drink plenty of clear fluids
2. Avoid solid food until feeling better
3. Try peppermint tea
4. Use a hot water bottle on tummy
5. Rest

Get urgent help (call 999 or go to A&E):
- Sudden, severe stomach pain
- Pain when touching your stomach
- Vomiting blood or dark vomit
- Blood in stools or black, tarry stools
- Can't urinate or pass stools
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain

## BACK PAIN

Common causes:
- Muscle strain
- Poor posture
- Sitting for long periods
- Lifting heavy objects incorrectly

Home treatment:
1. Stay as active as possible
2. Take painkillers (paracetamol, ibuprofen)
3. Use hot or cold compression packs
4. Gentle stretching
5. Maintain good posture

When to see a doctor:
- Pain doesn't improve after a few weeks
- Pain is severe or getting worse
- Numbness or tingling in legs or buttocks
- Loss of bladder or bowel control (call 999)

REMEMBER: These are general guidelines. If you're concerned about any symptoms, contact your GP or call NHS 24 on 111 for advice. In emergencies, always call 999.
