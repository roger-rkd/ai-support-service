HOME REMEDIES AND SELF-CARE GUIDE

## GENERAL SELF-CARE PRINCIPLES

1. Rest and Sleep
- Your body heals best when resting
- Aim for 7-9 hours sleep per night
- Take time off work if needed

2. Hydration
- Drink 6-8 glasses of water daily
- More if you have fever or diarrhea
- Warm drinks soothe sore throats and coughs

3. Nutrition
- Eat balanced diet with fruits and vegetables
- Light meals if feeling unwell
- Chicken soup genuinely helps colds!

4. Hygiene
- Wash hands regularly
- Cover mouth when coughing/sneezing
- Don't share towels or utensils when ill

## COMMON AILMENTS - HOME REMEDIES

### COLD REMEDIES
1. Honey and lemon in hot water (soothes throat, not for babies under 1)
2. Steam inhalation (helps clear congestion)
3. Saline nasal drops
4. Vitamin C from oranges, kiwis
5. Gargle warm salt water
6. Rest in warm, well-ventilated room

### HEADACHE RELIEF
1. Drink water (dehydration causes headaches)
2. Rest in quiet, dark room
3. Cold compress on forehead
4. Gentle head and neck massage
5. Paracetamol or ibuprofen
6. Peppermint oil on temples
7. Reduce screen time

### DIGESTIVE ISSUES
**Indigestion:**
- Peppermint tea
- Ginger (tea or biscuits)
- Small, frequent meals
- Avoid spicy, fatty foods
- Don't eat late at night

**Constipation:**
- Increase fiber (fruits, vegetables, whole grains)
- Drink plenty of water
- Exercise regularly
- Don't ignore urge to go
- Consider prunes or prune juice

**Diarrhea:**
- Drink plenty of fluids (water, diluted juice)
- Eat bland foods (toast, rice, bananas)
- Avoid dairy, fatty, spicy foods
- Rest

### MINOR CUTS AND SCRAPES
1. Wash hands first
2. Clean wound with clean water
3. Apply pressure with clean cloth if bleeding
4. Apply antiseptic cream
5. Cover with sterile dressing
6. Change dressing daily
7. Watch for signs of infection

### BRUISES
1. Apply ice pack wrapped in towel (15-20 minutes)
2. Rest and elevate affected area
3. Take paracetamol if painful
4. Arnica cream may help

### BURNS (MINOR)
1. Run cool (not cold) water over burn for 10-20 minutes
2. Remove jewelry/tight clothing before swelling
3. Cover with cling film or clean plastic bag
4. Don't use ice, butter, or creams
5. Take paracetamol if painful
6. Seek medical help if large, deep, or on face/hands

### INSECT BITES AND STINGS
1. Remove sting if visible (scrape, don't squeeze)
2. Wash area with soap and water
3. Apply cold compress
4. Take antihistamine for itching
5. Calamine lotion helps
6. Don't scratch

Seek urgent help if:
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Swelling of face, mouth, or throat
- Dizziness or feeling faint
- Widespread rash

### SUNBURN
1. Get out of sun immediately
2. Cool skin with cool water or bath
3. Apply after-sun lotion or aloe vera
4. Drink plenty of water
5. Take paracetamol or ibuprofen
6. Cover blisters; don't pop them

See doctor if:
- Severe blistering
- Fever and chills
- Feeling faint or dizzy

### INSOMNIA
1. Stick to regular sleep schedule
2. Create relaxing bedtime routine
3. Keep bedroom cool, dark, quiet
4. Avoid screens 1 hour before bed
5. No caffeine after 2pm
6. Don't lie awake - get up and do calm activity
7. Warm milk or chamomile tea
8. Write down worries to address tomorrow

### STRESS AND ANXIETY
1. Regular exercise (even short walks)
2. Breathing exercises (4-7-8: breathe in 4 counts, hold 7, out 8)
3. Talk to someone you trust
4. Write down feelings
5. Limit alcohol and caffeine
6. Practice mindfulness or meditation
7. Make time for hobbies
8. Adequate sleep

## FIRST AID KIT ESSENTIALS

Keep at home:
- Plasters (various sizes)
- Sterile gauze and bandages
- Antiseptic cream
- Paracetamol and ibuprofen
- Antihistamine tablets
- Thermometer
- Tweezers
- Scissors
- Safety pins
- Disposable gloves
- Hydrocortisone cream 1%
- Burn gel or dressing

## OVER-THE-COUNTER MEDICINES

Always read labels and follow instructions.

**Pain and fever:**
- Paracetamol (adults and children)
- Ibuprofen (adults and children over 3 months)
- Aspirin (adults only, not under 16)

**Colds and flu:**
- Decongestants
- Cough medicine
- Throat lozenges

**Allergies:**
- Antihistamines
- Hydrocortisone cream

**Digestive:**
- Antacids
- Laxatives (occasional use)
- Rehydration sachets

Ask pharmacist if:
- Not sure which medicine to use
- Taking other medications
- Pregnant or breastfeeding
- Medicine for children

## WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

Don't delay seeking help if:
- Symptoms are severe or worsening
- Home remedies not working after a few days
- You're concerned about symptoms
- You have underlying health conditions
- Symptoms affecting daily life

## EMERGENCY SYMPTOMS - CALL 999

- Chest pain
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding
- Suspected broken bone
- Severe allergic reaction
- Loss of consciousness
- Suspected stroke (FAST test)
- Seizure/fit
- Severe head injury

REMEMBER: These remedies are for minor, common ailments. Trust your instincts - if something feels seriously wrong, seek medical help. Call your GP, NHS 24 (111), or 999 in emergencies.
